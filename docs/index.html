Privacy Policy

Last updated July 11, 2026

Plato is a workout tracker. This policy explains what information the app collects, why, and what control you have over it. The short version: your data exists so the app can work for you — it is never sold, shared for advertising, or used for anything beyond running Plato.

What we collect

  • Account information — your email address, and a display name if you provide one, used to create and sign in to your account.
  • Fitness data you enter — workouts, exercises, sets, weights, reps, workout dates and durations, custom exercises, and templates.

Plato does not collect your location, contacts, photos, health data from Apple Health or other apps, or any advertising identifiers.

How it's used

Your data is used only to provide the app's features: syncing your workouts across devices, computing your statistics and personal records, and restoring your history when you sign in. There are no ads, no analytics profiles, and no sale or sharing of personal data with third parties for their own purposes.

Where it's stored

Plato stores your account and fitness data with Google Firebase (Authentication and Cloud Firestore), acting as our data processor. Data is transmitted over encrypted connections and protected by access rules so that only your signed-in account can read or write your own data. Firebase servers are located in the United States, so your data is processed there regardless of where you live. You can review Google's practices in the Firebase Privacy and Security documentation.

Like most connected services, Firebase temporarily processes technical information such as IP addresses and device identifiers to operate and secure the service (for example, to prevent abuse of sign-in). Plato does not access or store this information itself.

How long we keep it

Your data is kept for as long as your account exists. When you delete your account, your data is deleted immediately from the live database. Residual copies in encrypted backups are purged on the backup provider's standard schedule.

Preferences stored on your device

Small settings such as your preferred weight unit and rest-timer duration are stored locally on your device and never leave it.

Deleting your data

You can permanently delete your account and all associated data at any time, directly in the app: Profile → Delete account. This erases your workouts, templates, custom exercises, statistics, and the account itself. Deletion is immediate and cannot be undone.

Children

Plato is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them.
